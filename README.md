# 📦 Supply Chain Analytics Dashboard | Power BI

**Supply Chain Report | James Lai | 2026**

## 🎯 The Problem

When you work in logistics, data is everywhere. Stock counts, supplier orders, lead times, defect reports. But most of the time, it sits in separate files and no one has the full picture until something goes wrong. 

After 5 years on the ground managing inventory and stock reconciliation, I wanted to see if I could pull that picture together in one place. This dashboard is my attempt at that.

## 📊 What's Inside

Three pages, each focused on a different part of the supply chain.

### Overview
The big picture. Revenue, stock levels, product performance, and a quick look at which suppliers are pulling their weight.

### Product Insight
A close look at each product type. Order quantities, stock levels, lead times, and how price relates to how much is actually being sold.

### Supplier Insight
This is the one I find most useful. Which suppliers have the highest defect rates? Where are the availability gaps? How do costs break down across manufacturing and shipping? 

The Supplier Segmentation Matrix plots __profit margin against defect rate__. Each quadrant tells you what to do next:

| Quadrant | Margin | Defects | What It Means | Action |
|----------|--------|---------|---------------|--------|
| The Liability | Low | High | Expensive to manage, low returns | Phase out |
| The Critical Risk | High | High | High value but quality issues threaten operations | Fix or flip |
| The Reliable Utility | Low | Low | Efficient but low margin, commodity items | Automate |
| The Strategic Star | High | Low | Your best suppliers, high value with consistent quality | Expand and protect |
